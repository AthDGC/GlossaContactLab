About the Laboratory

The GlossaContact Laboratory is a research unit dedicated to the study of language contact phenomena, linguistic interference, and multilingualism. Based at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens, our lab investigates how languages influence each other through contact, borrowing, and code-switching.

Research Areas

Language Contact

Investigating patterns of linguistic influence between languages in contact situations

Linguistic Interference

Analyzing transfer phenomena and cross-linguistic influence in multilingual contexts

Code-Switching

Studying bilingual discourse and the alternation between languages in communication

Loanword Integration

Examining how borrowed lexical items are adapted into recipient languages

Historical Contact

Diachronic analysis of language contact in historical corpora

Computational Methods

Developing AI and corpus-based tools for contact linguistics research

Current Projects

Contact Detection in Diachronic Corpora

Developing computational methods to automatically identify and analyze language contact phenomena in historical texts. This project integrates with the CVL-CDSAML corpus and utilizes machine learning for pattern detection.

Multilingual Corpus Analysis

Comparative study of contact-induced change across multiple language pairs, with focus on Greek-English, Greek-Latin, and other Indo-European interactions.
